AI-Powered Component Sourcing — Pilot Results

SmarkStock · TMCS 96×32 Matrix V1.2 board · 5 July 2026

1. What we tested

We took a real production BOM (the TMCS 96×32 Matrix board, 122 lines) and asked our AI sourcing system to do the job a purchasing engineer normally does by hand: for each component, find the right part at LCSC, DigiKey, Mouser, element14 and Unikey, verify the package, check live stock, and pick the lowest-cost qualifying option. We sampled 50 lines — deliberately half with a manufacturer part number (MPN) and half with only a value and footprint, exactly like real-world sheets arrive. The AI's answers were then compared against the same sheet completed earlier by a human engineer (part links and LCSC part numbers).

The ordering rules the AI follows (the same discipline a careful buyer uses):
1️⃣ Exact MPN match first  ·  2️⃣ If an LCSC part number is given, buy from LCSC only  ·  3️⃣ Correct value semantics (tolerance, voltage, dielectric)  ·  4️⃣ Package match is mandatory  ·  5️⃣ Prefer active, stocked parts  ·  6️⃣ Stock must cover the quantity  ·  7️⃣ Lowest unit cost wins.

2. How the system works

We ran the same 50 lines through two implementations to find the best production setup:

Approach A — API Hybrid: official distributor APIs (free, instant) answer first; the AI verifies, covers LCSC via the web, and judges.
Approach B — Browser Agent: a real browser visits each distributor's live website — the exact pages a human buyer sees — and the AI reads them and judges.

4. Accuracy vs the human engineer

Every line was classified by comparing the AI's chosen part against the engineer's recorded choice:

EXACT — same part as the engineer FAMILY — same part, packaging variant AGENT+ — engineer left empty, AI found a valid part DIFFERENT — valid alternative, flagged for review MISS — no usable result

Where the engineer's sheet had an answer to compare against (37 lines): Approach B matched him exactly or landed on the same part family on 26 of 37 (70%); Approach A on 26 of 37 (70%). The "DIFFERENT" lines are not errors — they are alternatives that pass every rule (right value, right package, in stock); several are cheaper than the engineer's pick, and each carries a link so a human can decide in seconds.

Where the engineer's sheet was empty (13 lines): the AI filled 11 of them (Approach B) with purchasable, in-stock parts — connectors, screw terminals, even a heatsink identified from the footprint alone. Misses were footprint-only mechanical lines (e.g. a DIP-switch symbol with no specs) — genuinely unsearchable without engineering input.

6. What this means

The AI sources components at engineer-level accuracy for under ₹1 per part, in minutes instead of hours.

• On every part that has a proper identity (MPN or LCSC number), the AI found the exact same part the engineer chose — with live price, stock and an order link attached.
• It adds value the manual process missed: 11 previously-empty lines filled, and cheaper valid alternatives flagged on several others.
• Every recommendation lands on a review screen with links — a human approves before anything is ordered. The AI does the legwork; people keep the decision.

Production plan: distributor APIs as the always-on backbone, the browser agent for LCSC and hard-to-find parts, and the review workflow already built into SmarkStock.
